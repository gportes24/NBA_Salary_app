The PER

The National Basketball Association has been in this game for a while - and understands the value of statisics. They have calucluated a score termed "Player Efficiency Rating" (hereafter PER), which measures the players' per-minute productivity. The considerations within this score is too complicated to discuss here however the scale is generally between 4 and 30 (the difference between a good high school player at low end and LeBron James setting the standard near the top). While comprehensive, the PER does not provide a lifetime judgement of the fundamental value of a player, but essentially, according to ESPN Analyst John Hollinger, "summarize a player's statistical accomplishments in a single number." And voila, a main component of our job had been done for us by those handy NBA officials. We could now get to work scoring the PER against other statistics and salary metrix.

The Stats

We calculated the salary contracted for each NBA player for the 2019-2020 season and cross-referenced them against their statisitics. These included points scored, blocks, assists, rebounds, fouls, minutes played and games played. Each of these provided additional context for the player's PER. Our Statistics webpage allows the viewer to analyze the salaries of players by team and their PER.

The Starting money

One of the keys to components understanding NBA teams is understanding the salary of a team. Looking over a three-year move through their payments and the money they paid towards their salary caps provides an initial understanding of how to break things down across players.
